Mountain Weather · Mid-May
~40–50% rain chance daily
Variable conditions

Mountain weather changes fast — clear mornings can turn to afternoon showers. Rain gear is essential, not optional. Snow possible above 1,800m. Check Météo France each morning. Bad forecast = rest day at the Airbnb.

Daylight · May 19–22

Long days but mountains block direct sun earlier — expect shade on east-facing slopes by 7 PM. Morning light on the Cirque de Lescun is spectacular.

Layering formula: Base layer + fleece + rain shell. Mornings start cold (low 40s), sun warms fast, then clouds can roll in. Pack for the full 43–68°F range. Warm hat and gloves not overkill for 6 AM mountain air.

Day 8 · Tuesday, May 20

Cirque de Lescun & Settling In

First full day in the mountains. The anchor is a walk with views of the Cirque de Lescun — the dramatic limestone amphitheater that makes this village special. One good walk, then rest. Let the mountains do the work.

8:00–9:00 AM

Wake up to mountain air. Espresso from the machine at La Bourdette. Mornings here are quiet and cool — bundle Lucy up. The south-facing terrace has mountain views — this is peak quality of life.

9:30 AM – 12:00 PM core

Walk to a Cirque de Lescun viewpoint. There are several trails from the village that provide stunning views of the cirque — the ring of limestone peaks (Pic d'Anie, Pic Billare, Les Orgues de Camplong) that surround Lescun's valley. Options depend on trail conditions:

  • Village to Pont de Lamary (~1.5 hrs round trip): Gentle path along the valley floor. Mostly flat, good for the carrier. Views open up as you walk south from the village.
  • Plateau de Lhers (~2 hrs round trip): Walk or short drive to the Lhers plateau for wide-open views of the cirque and the Pic d'Anie. Slightly more elevation gain but manageable with a carrier.
  • Village loop: Simply walk through Lescun village and out along the road toward the cirque. The views start almost immediately — you don't need to go far.

Lucy rides in the carrier. Bring snacks, water, extra layers, and rain gear. Turn back if the weather shifts or Lucy is unhappy — no summit goals here.

Trail conditions: Mid-May in the Pyrenees means some trails may still be muddy or have residual snow at higher elevations. Stick to lower trails (below 1,200m) and check conditions at the Lescun mairie (town hall) or ask locals. Trail runners or light hiking boots recommended — not city sneakers.
12:00–1:00 PM

Lunch at La Bourdette or at one of Lescun's restaurants (1–2 options in the village, 2 min walk). The kitchen is fully equipped — gas stove, double oven, dishwasher. Provisions from Oloron-Sainte-Marie may be needed — Lescun itself has very limited shopping. A small épicerie might be open seasonally.

1:00–4:00 PM

Lucy nap — the crib and room-darkening shades are already set up. Your nap. Read. Sit on the south-facing terrace. Watch the clouds move across the peaks. This is the point of going to a mountain village — the stillness.

4:30–6:00 PM if energy allows

Short evening stroll through the village (2 min walk) or the fields below. Late afternoon light on the cirque is spectacular. Or just sit on the terrace and watch it from La Bourdette.

7:00 PM

Dinner at La Bourdette. Cook with whatever you picked up in Oloron — the kitchen is excellent (gas stove, double oven). Or fire up the BBQ grill if the weather is good. Mountain food: hearty soups, cheese, bread, wine. Simple and warm.

Bad Weather Day: Stay at La Bourdette. Read by the fireplace. Play with Lucy — the baby gates are already in place. Drive down to Oloron-Sainte-Marie for a café lunch and market shopping. The mountains will be there tomorrow.
Day 9 · Wednesday, May 21

A Second Walk & Mountain Quiet

Another day in the mountains. Tomorrow you drive to San Sebastián, so today is the last day of true quiet. One walk, then recovery mode.

9:00 AM – 12:00 PM core

Morning walk. Try a different trail than yesterday, or revisit the same one — the light will be different. Options:

  • Walk toward Le Lac de Lhurs: A longer option (~3–4 hrs round trip) if conditions are good and you're feeling strong. Beautiful mountain lake. Carrier essential. Only attempt in good weather.
  • Drive to Col de la Pierre Saint-Martin: ~45 min drive up to a high pass (~1,760m) with panoramic views into Spain. The road is dramatic and the views are enormous. You don't need to hike — the views are from the parking area. Check that the road is open (sometimes closed into mid-May due to snow).
  • Repeat yesterday's route: No shame in doing the same walk twice. Lucy won't mind, and neither should you.
12:30 PM

Lunch. Do a load of laundry in the washer (no dryer — use the drying rack or hang outside if sunny). Start thinking about tomorrow's drive to San Sebastián. Pack partially. Fill the gas tank if you haven't already — there are no gas stations in Lescun.

Afternoon

Rest. Recovery before tomorrow's drive. Read. Play with Lucy on the terrace or take out the bikes for a short spin if feeling adventurous. Enjoy the last mountain evening.

Evening

Dinner at La Bourdette. BBQ on the terrace if the weather holds, or cook inside. Open the last bottle of the Saint-Émilion wine you bought. Watch the sun set on the cirque.

Tomorrow's Drive: Lescun to San Sebastián is ~2.5 hours (180 km). Route: descend the Aspe Valley → Oloron → head south via the Col du Somport or west to the E80/A64 → cross into Spain near Irún or via Pamplona. The Pamplona route is slightly longer but on better roads. Stop in Pamplona for lunch if you have time.

Practical Notes — Lescun

Getting There & Away

From Saint-Émilion (~3.5 hrs): A65 south to Pau, then D-roads into the Aspe Valley. The last 30 minutes are on narrow mountain roads — beautiful but slow. Not stressful, just scenic.

To San Sebastián (~2.5 hrs): Descend to Oloron, then either cross via the Col du Somport (mountain pass, beautiful, slower) or take the A64/E80 west and cross at the coast near Irún. Google Maps will route you — download offline maps for the Pyrenees as cell coverage is spotty.

Supplies & Groceries

  • Lescun has almost nothing. There may be a seasonal épicerie and you can pick up fresh bread from the village (2 min walk), but don't count on more than that. Stock up before you arrive.
  • Oloron-Sainte-Marie (35 min drive): This is your supply town. It has a Carrefour, pharmacy, bakery, and a good Saturday market (if you arrive on a Saturday). Stock up on everything here — diapers, wipes, groceries, wine, BBQ supplies.
  • Water: Bring bottled water from the lowlands. Mountain tap water is usually excellent but check with Thierry.
  • Gas: Fill up in Oloron or Bedous (village in the Aspe Valley). There is no gas station in Lescun.
  • Laundry: La Bourdette has a washer (no dryer). Plan to do a load of baby clothes here — this is your laundry stop since the Saint-Émilion Logis had no washer. Use the drying rack or hang outside on a sunny day.

Hiking with a 9-Month-Old — Mountain Edition

  • Carrier only. Stroller is useless on mountain trails. Bring a good hiking carrier with sun/rain cover.
  • Pack for Lucy: Extra warm layer (fleece + hat), sun protection, snacks, water, dummy/pacifier. Changes happen fast in the mountains.
  • Turn-back time is real. If weather changes, Lucy fusses, or the trail gets sketchy, turn around. No trail is worth a bad experience at 9 months.
  • Morning hikes are best. Weather is more predictable, Lucy is happiest, and afternoon thunderstorms are common in the Pyrenees in May.
  • Cell coverage: Spotty to nonexistent on trails. Download offline maps. Tell someone your route and expected return time.
  • Footwear: Trail runners or light hiking boots. The trails around Lescun are rocky and potentially muddy in mid-May. City sneakers will leave you slipping.

What to Eat & Drink

  • Garbure: Traditional Pyrenean soup — thick, hearty, made with cabbage, beans, duck confit, and whatever vegetables are seasonal. Perfect after a mountain walk.
  • Ossau-Iraty: AOC Basque/Béarnais sheep's milk cheese. Buy it from any market or épicerie in the valley. Pair with cherry jam (confiture de cerises noires) — the local tradition.
  • Jurançon wine: The local white, from vineyards near Pau. Dry (sec) or sweet (moelleux). Try the dry version with cheese — surprisingly excellent.
  • Mountain cooking at La Bourdette: The kitchen is excellent — gas stove, double oven, espresso machine, dishwasher. Keep it simple though. The best mountain meals are bread, cheese, soup, wine. Fire up the BBQ on a clear evening.
