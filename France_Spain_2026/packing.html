Packing & Planning

13 Nights · Paris → Wine Country → Mountains → Coast

Optimized for 13 nights across four climates: city (Paris, 50–66°F), wine country (Saint-Émilion, 55–72°F), mountains (Lescun, 45–65°F), and coast (San Sebastián, 52–68°F). You'll be in a mix of hotels and Airbnbs, with a car for the second half. Pack for layers and rain — every location has a 30–50% daily rain chance in May.

Strategy: one checked bag each + one carry-on each + Doona gate-checked. Buy consumables locally (diapers, wipes, baby food, toiletries). French pharmacies and Monoprix are better stocked than you think.

Travel Documents

  • Passports — all three (check Lucy's expiry date well in advance)
  • Flight confirmations / boarding passes (DEN→CDG, AF58 return)
  • Train tickets — all INOUI reservations (Paris→Bordeaux, Biarritz→Paris)
  • Hotel / Airbnb confirmations (Le Cinq Codet, Saint-Émilion Airbnb, Lescun Airbnb, San Sebastián lodging, CDG airport hotel)
  • Rental car reservation + driver's license (international driver's permit usually not required for US citizens in France/Spain, but carry one if you have it)
  • Travel insurance documents (confirm coverage for France + Spain, baby included)
  • Lucy's birth certificate (some airlines require for lap infants)
  • Credit cards with no foreign transaction fees (minimum 2 — backup matters)
  • Euros in cash (€200–300 for small vendors, markets, rural areas where cards may not work)
  • Digital copies of everything (email to yourselves + cloud backup)
  • G7 Taxi app installed and account set up (for Paris)
  • SNCF Connect app (for train tickets / schedule changes)
  • Google Maps offline maps downloaded — Île-de-France, Nouvelle-Aquitaine, Pyrénées-Atlantiques, Basque Country (Spain)

Adult Clothing — Him

Temperature range across the trip: 45–73°F. Layering is everything. Mountain mornings will be cold; Paris afternoons can be warm.

  • 4–5 tops (merino wool base layers are ideal — they regulate temperature, resist odor, pack small. 2 merino tees + 1 merino long-sleeve + 1 casual button-down)
  • 2 technical travel pants (stretch, wrinkle-resistant, quick-dry — Outlier, Western Rise, or similar. One darker pair for dinners, one lighter for hiking)
  • 1 pair shorts or convertible pants (for warm days in Saint-Émilion or the coast)
  • 1 flannel or midweight shirt (evening layer, casual dinners)
  • 1 lightweight micropuff or synthetic insulated jacket (for Lescun mornings and mountain evenings — Patagonia Nano Puff or equivalent)
  • 1 packable rain shell (Gore-Tex or similar — non-negotiable. You will use it)
  • 5 sets underwear (merino if you have them — pack less, wash less)
  • 5 pairs socks (2 merino hiking, 3 regular)
  • 1 light scarf or buff (warmth + wind protection)
  • 1 baseball cap or sun hat
  • Sunglasses
  • Sleepwear
  • Swimsuit (Le Cinq Codet spa + San Sebastián beach)
  • Shoes: 2 pairs max. City sneaker that's comfortable for all-day walking (Allbirds, New Balance 990, etc.) + trail runner for Lescun hikes (Salomon, Hoka, Brooks — something with grip). Trail runners double as bad-weather city shoes.

  • 2 warm layers (fleece zip-up, sweater)
  • 1 warm jacket or bunting for mountain mornings
  • Sun hat (wide brim)
  • Warm hat (for Lescun — 45°F mornings are real)
  • 5–6 pairs socks (they will vanish — accept this)
  • Sleep sack
  • PJs (3 sets)
  • Swim diaper (for the spa hot tub if allowed — check with Le Cinq Codet)

Hiking — Lescun / Pyrenees

You're doing moderate walks, not technical climbs. But the terrain is mountain terrain — muddy trails, rocky patches, uneven ground. Gear up properly.

  • Trail runners with good grip (see shoes above — these serve double duty)
  • Trekking poles (optional but very helpful on descents, especially with baby weight in front carrier — collapsible/packable poles)
  • Small daypack (20–25L — carries water, snacks, baby gear, extra layer)
  • Water bottles (2 — refill in Lescun before heading out. No water sources on most trails)
  • Trail snacks (nuts, bars, fruit — for adults. Baby snacks separate)
  • Rain shell (you already have this — see clothing)
  • Warm layer (micropuff — you already have this)
  • Hat + sunglasses (mountain sun is stronger at altitude)
  • Sunscreen (reapply every 2 hours at altitude)
  • Baby carrier with sun shade / rain cover capability
  • Extra outfit for Lucy in the pack (weather changes fast)

Tech & Misc

  • Universal power adapter — France uses Type C/E (two round pins). Spain uses the same. Bring 2 adapters (one for bedroom, one for common area / car charger won't help at an Airbnb).
  • Portable phone charger / battery pack (20,000 mAh — will save you on long driving days and hikes)
  • Phone charging cables (USB-C, Lightning — whatever you need, plus one extra)
  • Car phone mount (for GPS during driving legs — clip-on or magnetic)
  • Small packable tote bag (for daily outings, market runs, pintxos crawl)
  • Laundry detergent pods (for Airbnb washing machines — those small pods from Amazon, 6–8 is plenty)
  • Ziplock bags (multiple sizes — the most versatile travel item ever made)
  • Pen (for customs forms)
  • Small notebook (for wine tasting notes in Saint-Émilion — or use your phone)

Lodging Notes by Location

  • Paris — Le Cinq Codet (3 nights): Provides robes, slippers, high-end toiletries, hairdryer. Request baby crib and bottle warmer in advance. Spa suite has hot tub — bring swimsuits. Don't overpack toiletries; the hotel has you covered.
  • Saint-Émilion — Logis de Colette (3 nights): No washer/dryer. Full kitchen (pots, pans, dishes, cooking basics). Travel crib (Pack 'n Play) provided. Free parking. Outdoor dining area. WiFi. Heating + fireplace. Hosted by Xavier — he greets you on arrival. Includes a tasting at Couvent des Jacobins (Grand Cru Classé). Stock up at Carrefour Libourne on the way in.
  • Lescun — La Bourdette (3 nights): Renovated sheepfold. 4 bedrooms, 2 baths. Baby gear on-site: crib, Pack 'n Play, high chair with straps, baby safety gates, room-darkening shades. Washer (no dryer — drying rack provided) — this is your laundry stop. Full kitchen (gas stove, double oven, espresso machine, dishwasher). BBQ grill, bikes, south-facing terrace with mountain views. Free parking. Self check-in (lockbox). This is the most remote stop — stock up on everything at Carrefour in Oloron-Sainte-Marie on the way up. Nearest pharmacy is 35 min away.
  • San Sebastián — Lodging (3 nights): Not yet booked. Same checklist: confirm crib, kitchen, washing machine. Central location (Parte Vieja or Gros) preferred for walkability.
  • CDG Airport Hotel (1 night): Yotel or Sheraton. Keep one change of clothes and toiletries in a small bag accessible without unpacking everything. You're here for sleep only.

Risk Mitigation

Things to sort before departure.

Flight Cancellation
Travel insurance that covers trip interruption + medical. Confirm the policy covers all three travelers including an infant.
Lost Luggage
Pack one full day of clothes + all medications + baby essentials in the carry-on. If checked bags are delayed, you can survive 24–48 hours.
Car Trouble
Rental car includes roadside assistance (confirm with agency). Save the agency's emergency number in your phone. Keep the rental contract in the glove box.
Medical Emergency
Emergency: 112 (EU-wide), 15 (SAMU — French medical), 091/092 (Spain). Nearest hospitals noted on each destination page. Travel insurance card accessible.
Lucy Gets Sick
French pharmacists can diagnose and recommend treatment for common infant issues (fever, stomach, rash). No appointment needed. Pediatrician ("pédiatre") if needed — ask the pharmacist for a referral.
Passport Loss
Digital copies of all passports saved to email + cloud. US Embassy Paris: +33 1 43 12 22 22 (for after-hours emergencies). US Consulate Barcelona handles Spain region.

Pre-Departure Checklist

2–4 Weeks Before

  • Confirm all lodging reservations (hotel + 3 Airbnbs + CDG hotel)
  • Book INOUI trains if not already done (Paris→Bordeaux, Biarritz→Paris)
  • Finalize rental car booking (confirm: automatic transmission, pickup Bordeaux Saint-Jean, drop-off Biarritz)
  • Check passport expiry dates — all three passports must be valid 3+ months beyond return date
  • Confirm travel insurance coverage (France + Spain, infant included)
  • Set up G7 Taxi app + SNCF Connect app
  • Download offline maps (Google Maps) — Île-de-France, Nouvelle-Aquitaine, Pyrénées-Atlantiques, Basque Country (Spain)
  • Notify credit card companies of travel dates (France + Spain, May 13–26)
  • Check credit card CDW coverage for French/Spanish rental cars
  • Contact Le Cinq Codet: request baby crib + bottle warmer
  • Contact Airbnb hosts: confirm crib, high chair, washing machine, kitchen, heating
  • Break in hiking shoes if new

Day Before Departure

  • Charge all devices (phones, battery pack, camera, baby monitor, sound machine)
  • Check in online for the outbound flight
  • Print or save boarding passes, train tickets, rental car confirmation
  • Final passport check — all three in the carry-on
  • Pack carry-on with: 1 day of clothes each, all medications, baby essentials for 24 hours, chargers, documents
  • Pack Doona for gate-check
  • Set a weather alert for Paris (first morning) — know if you need a layer at CDG
