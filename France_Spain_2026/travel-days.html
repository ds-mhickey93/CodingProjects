Arrival · Tuesday, May 13

CDG → Paris Hotel

11:05 AM core

Arrive at CDG. Budget 30–45 minutes for passport control (EU entry) and baggage claim. Have your passports ready — all three, including Lucy's.

12:00–1:30 PM core

Option A — Taxi (~50–70 min, €55–65): G7 app or taxi stand at arrivals. Flat rate to Left Bank. Door-to-door, no transfers. Book with "siège bébé" for car seat.

Option B — RER B (~1h15, €11.45/adult): CDG Terminal 2 → Denfert-Rochereau → Métro Line 6 → La Motte-Picquet–Grenelle → walk to hotel. Lucy rides free under 4. Trade-off: stairs, turnstiles, and a transfer with luggage.

Decision: If Lucy is calm, taxi. If she's fussy about the car seat, train. Read the room at CDG.

Jet Lag Strategy: You'll arrive having traveled overnight from Denver. Push through to early evening if you can. Get outside, walk, eat lunch. Put Lucy down at her normal bedtime (Paris time). The spa suite hot tub helps. Don't nap past 3 PM or you'll be up at 2 AM.
Transit · Friday, May 16

Paris → Bordeaux (INOUI)

8:30 AM

Check out of Le Cinq Codet. Have everything packed the night before. Quick breakfast at the hotel or grab a pastry from Boulangerie Secco on Rue Jean Nicot.

9:15–9:30 AM core

Taxi (G7) from hotel to Gare Montparnasse. ~12–15 minutes. Book 10 min ahead. Metro alternative: École Militaire (Line 8) → La Motte-Picquet (Line 6) → Montparnasse. ~15 min, but stairs with luggage.

9:45–10:05 AM core

Arrive at the station. Find the departure board — platform announced ~20 min before departure. Look for the correct hall (Hall 1 or Hall 2). First Class coaches are usually at one end of the train — check your car number on the ticket.

INOUI First Class Tips:
  • First Class seats are wider, quieter, and have power outlets at every seat
  • Book seats facing each other with a table ("carré") — gives you space to spread out with baby gear
  • There is a family/quiet car on some trains — check when booking if available
  • Luggage areas at the end of each coach — large bags go there. Keep essentials in a smaller bag at your seat
  • There's no dedicated baby-changing area on most INOUI trains, but the accessible toilet (larger stall) works
  • Children under 4 ride free — no ticket needed for Lucy
  • Bar car has drinks, snacks, and usually coffee. Opens after departure
10:17 AM core

Train departs Montparnasse. ~2 hours 10 minutes to Bordeaux Saint-Jean. Settle in. Lucy will likely nap — the train motion is soothing. Enjoy the French countryside rolling past.

~12:30 PM

Arrive Bordeaux Saint-Jean. Follow signs to the car rental area. See Saint-Émilion page for car pickup and drive details.

Car Rental — What to Know

  • Pickup: Bordeaux Saint-Jean station. Most agencies in the parking structure or north exit. Budget 30–45 min for paperwork.
  • Drop-off: Biarritz station (Gare de Biarritz). Confirm the drop-off location when booking — some agencies require different return points.
  • Car type: Compact crossover SUV (Renault Symbioz, Peugeot 2008, or similar). Enough for luggage + baby gear + car seat. Not too big for narrow village streets.
  • Transmission: Request automatic. Manual is still common in France — double-check when booking.
  • Fuel: Most rental cars take diesel (gazole). Confirm at pickup. Return with a full tank.
  • Tolls: French autoroutes have tolls. Budget ~€30–50 total for the trip. Most accept credit cards. Some booths are automated (Télépéage lanes) — use the "CB" (carte bancaire) lane.
  • Insurance: Your credit card may cover CDW (collision damage waiver). Check before the trip. Decline the rental agency's super CDW if you're covered.
  • GPS: Download offline maps (Google Maps) for Nouvelle-Aquitaine + Basque Country before the trip. Cell coverage is spotty in the Pyrenees.
  • Speed limits: Autoroute 130 km/h (110 in rain), national roads 80 km/h, towns 50 km/h. Speed cameras are everywhere.
  • Right of way: "Priorité à droite" — vehicles entering from the right have priority at unmarked intersections. Watch for this in villages.

Driving with Lucy

  • Car seat: Bring the Doona (clicks into base) or install your own. Rental agency seats are hit-or-miss quality.
  • Stop every 1.5–2 hours. Lucy needs out. You need to stretch. Plan stops proactively — aires (rest areas) on autoroutes work, or small town centers for coffee.
  • Snacks and entertainment: Keep baby snacks, a bottle, and a few toys within reach from the back seat. Music helps.
  • Morning departures: Start drives in the morning when Lucy is freshest. She'll likely nap for the first 30–60 minutes.
Transit · Sunday, May 25

San Sebastián → Biarritz → Paris

The big travel day. Drive to Biarritz, drop the car, and take the INOUI back to Paris. You'll arrive in Paris late evening and head to a CDG airport hotel for the night.

8:00–9:00 AM

Final pack. Check out of the lodging. Load the car one last time. Fill gas tank if not already done.

9:00–9:35 AM core

Drive from San Sebastián to Biarritz. ~35 minutes on the A-8/E-70 coastal motorway. Cross the border (no checkpoint within Schengen). Follow signs to Gare de Biarritz.

9:45–10:15 AM core

Return the car at the rental agency near Biarritz station. Budget 15–20 min for inspection and paperwork. Remove the car seat, all luggage, and check for any personal items. Walk to the station.

Check your ticket core

Board the INOUI from Biarritz to Paris. ~4.5 hours. The train may go to Paris Montparnasse or directly — confirm when booking. If the train goes to Montparnasse, you'll need to get from Montparnasse to your CDG hotel afterward (taxi or RER).

Booking tip: Try to book a train that arrives at a convenient time for getting to CDG. A train arriving around 3–4 PM gives you time for the CDG transfer without rushing. Check if any Biarritz→CDG direct options exist (some TGV routes serve the airport station).
~3:00–4:00 PM (estimated)

Arrive in Paris. If at Montparnasse, take a taxi to CDG (~1 hr, €55–65) or RER B from Denfert-Rochereau (~50 min, €11.45). If the train serves CDG directly, you're already there.

Departure · Monday, May 26

CDG → Home

10:30–11:00 AM core

Baby gate-check: If using the Doona, gate-check it. Ask at the check-in counter for a gate-check tag. Carry Lucy through the airport in the carrier or Doona in stroller mode, then hand it off at the jet bridge. It'll be waiting on the jet bridge when you land in Denver.
